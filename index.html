ConcreteGuard: Deformation Analysis & VR Visualization of DFAB HOUSE

Research Project ETH Zürich · Geosensors and Engineering Geodesy (GSEG) · Dec 2024 – Sep 2025

The DFAB HOUSE is a landmark of digital fabrication built in 2018/19 but never monitored long-term. Within the ConcreteGuard project, I turned six years of concrete structural change into a millimeter-accurate digital twin and an interactive VR product that makes point-cloud analysis explorable for engineers, researchers, and visitors alike.

  • Built a scan-to-digital-twin pipeline: acquired multi-epoch TLS and photogrammetric imagery of the DFAB HOUSE, registered 2024/2025 scans against the 2019 as-built baseline at 2–4 mm accuracy, and derived deformation via Cloud-to-Mesh (C2M) analysis.
  • Detected and explained millimeter-scale structural change: localized sinking (−12.5 mm, confirmed by visible cracks) and rising (+7.7 mm) of the SmartSlab ceiling, and worked with structural engineers to trace them to concrete creep and overestimated prestressing, turning point-cloud measurements into engineering decisions.
  • Productized the results as an interactive digital twin in VR: a Unity application for Meta Quest 3 combining 3D Gaussian Splatting with laser-scan meshes colored by camera imagery, together with the deformation maps and Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) results. Used for research, stakeholder communication, and visitor engagement.
  • Balanced core 3D-industry trade-offs: blended TLS and camera-reconstructed point clouds in RealityCapture and reconstructed high-fidelity models in Leica Cyclone and Blender, optimizing geometric accuracy against real-time rendering performance on standalone VR hardware.
  • Delivered a transferable workflow: presented results and a live VR demo at the project closing meeting; the scan–analyze–visualize pipeline is reusable for other digitally fabricated concrete structures.

Alpine Geomonitoring with Terrestrial Laser Scanner Point Clouds

Master's Thesis ETH Zürich · Geosensors and Engineering Geodesy (GSEG) · Oct 2023 – Feb 2024

Climate change is accelerating landslides and rock falls in the Alps. In this thesis, I quantified multi-year surface deformation below the Breithorn and Gugla mountains (Mattertal, Valais) from high-resolution terrestrial laser scanner (TLS) point clouds, and validated the results against permanent GNSS stations, benchmarking which point-cloud change-detection methods are accurate and reliable enough for operational monitoring solutions.

  • Applied and compared two 3D change detection algorithms, M3C2 and the learning-based F2S3, across scans spanning July 2019 to September 2022, detecting six active regions including the Gugla block glacier and the Lägenschnee–Europaweg slope.
  • Validated F2S3 displacement vectors against GNSS ground truth: median deviations of 3.4° in dip angle, 4.5° in dip direction, and 0.05 m in magnitude within rigid-motion areas.
  • Reduced deformation-estimation uncertainty by ~20% (MAD 0.20 m → 0.16 m) by averaging multiple scans per epoch, and compared RANSAC+ICP vs. plane-based ICP registration methods.
  • Filtered vegetation with a pretrained PointNet++ classifier and explored UAV photogrammetry (Structure-from-Motion) as a complementary data source for deformation monitoring.
📄 Publication: Shi, N., Medic, T., Meyer, N., Voordendag, A., & Wieser, A. (2025). Quantifying and Reducing the Uncertainty of 3D Displacement Estimates from Terrestrial Laser Scanner Point Clouds: A Case Study in Alpine Geomonitoring. Proceedings of the 6th Joint International Symposium on Deformation Monitoring (JISDM 2025), Karlsruhe, Germany.

Parking Space Extraction from Orthophotos with Deep Learning

Internship Project TU Wien · Institute of Transportation · Aug – Sep 2023

Complete, up-to-date parking-space data is scarce, yet vital for traffic management and urban planning. Working with Austria-wide 20 cm orthophotos (BEV), I trained semantic segmentation models to map on-street and off-street parking across Vienna, turning raw aerial imagery into a geospatial data product that answers a concrete planning need.

  • Built the end-to-end PyTorch training pipeline: tiling 5000×5000-px orthophotos into 224×224 patches, spatial clustering for unbiased train/val/test splits, focal loss, and morphological post-processing of predictions.
  • Curated multi-class ground truth (non-/on-/off-street parking) by fusing the Vienna multi-purpose survey map with OpenStreetMap in QGIS and ArcGIS Pro, using layer intersection to filter incorrect OSM labels.
  • Trained Dense-U-Net-DLA and SkipFuse-Dense-U-Net architectures fusing RGB imagery with OSM masks; improved IoU from 0.42 to 0.51 through class-weighted training, at 0.97 pixel accuracy.
  • Documented the full workflow, data, and reproducible training instructions for handover to the research group.
📄 Publication: Ureña Pliego, M., Martínez Marín, R., Shi, N., Shibayama, T., Leth, U., & Marchamalo Sacristán, M. (2025). Transport-Related Surface Detection with Machine Learning: Analyzing Temporal Trends in Madrid and Vienna. Remote Sensing Applications: Society and Environment, 37, 101503.
